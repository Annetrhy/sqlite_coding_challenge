Task 1 — Top 5 Customers by Lifetime Spend:
This finds the five customers who have spent the most overall. I calculated totals by multiplying quantity and price for each item, then added everything up per customer. This gives a good idea of who the most valuable customers are. The results show that a small group of customers makes up a big portion of total sales, which suggests the business relies heavily on repeat or high-spending buyers. Since there’s no clear data on returns or discounts, these numbers represent total (gross) spending.
Task 2 — Revenue by Product Category:
This looks at how much money each product category brings in. I grouped sales by category and added up all item totals. I also made a version that only includes “Delivered” orders to see how actual completed sales compare. Some categories stay at the top either way, while others drop when only delivered orders are counted. This shows that not all sales are finalized and that fulfillment can affect which categories are truly performing best.
Task 3 — Employees Earning Above Department Average:
This shows which employees make more than the average salary in their department. I first found the average salary for each department, then compared each employee to that number. The results highlight pay gaps within departments and show that some employees are earning significantly more than their peers. Departments with only one employee don’t appear here because there’s no one to compare them to.
Task 4 — Cities with the Most Gold Customers (and Distribution):
This ranks cities based on how many Gold-level customers they have. I also cleaned up the city data by fixing formatting and replacing missing values with “Unknown.” On top of that, I broke down each city by Gold, Silver, and Bronze customers to see overall loyalty patterns. Some cities clearly have stronger customer loyalty, while others have more lower-tier customers, which could be a growth opportunity. If cities had the same number of Gold customers, they were sorted alphabetically.
Tooling & Validation:
I completed everything using Visual Studio Code with SQLite. I tested each query one at a time to make sure it worked and double-checked totals and counts against the original tables. I also cleaned up messy data using functions like COALESCE, LOWER, and TRIM. Along the way, I fixed small errors like missing semicolons until everything ran smoothly.\
